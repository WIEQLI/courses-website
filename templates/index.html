who

Bio here. Doug is a professor of Geophysics, director of the Geophysical Inversion Facility (GIF) and world leader in geophysical inversions. He is dedicated to making geophysics more useful in its application for solving problems of relevance to society. Motivated to make geophysics more accessible and engaging, he has and continues to lead efforts for distributing software codes and learning resource material for students and practising geoscientists. In 2017, Doug will be on tour presenting the SEG Distinguished Instructor Short Course with the intention of promoting fundamental understanding about principles of electromagnetics and how the different surveys make use of these principles to tackle a broad spectrum of problems using EM geophysics.

course

My goal is to inspire geoscientists to use EM geophysics to help solve their applied problem and for them to have an understanding about the type of survey they might employ, the underlying physical principles, and a realistic expectation for what information they might be able to obtain. Case histories provide a unifying link and I draw from an extensive set of examples from resource exploration and environmental and geotechnical areas The surveys in the case histories may use steady state fields (DC), harmonic waves in frequency ( FDEM), or time domain signals (TDEM). Natural or controlled sources are used and surveys can be carried out in the air, on the earth’s surface or underground. The course attempts to explore as many surveys as possible within the range of case histories that are most relevant to the site at which the DISC is presented.

The learning elements of this course will not arise from slides of intensive mathematics but rather from illustrated examples about the problem, EM fundamentals, the survey, and the outcome. The ability to interact with fundamentals, which are key to understanding a survey, is facilitated by interactive apps that are run in notebooks. Visualization of fields is a necessary element in linking EM fundamentals with a chosen EM survey and the apps allow the user to ask “what if” questions and, in real time, obtain an image; this leads to a powerful way of understanding physical phenomena.

Electromagnetic geophysics is a large field and it is not possible to cover all possible applications and surveys, even at a non-technical level. However, in one day we can provide you with insight about how to assess the applicability of EM for your problem and show some successful examples. An open-source web-site, Geosci.xyz can serve as a portal to further information, understanding and practicalities.

Fundamentals

Maxwell’s equations connect EM fields with physical properties, sigma, mu, epsilon. These are diagnostic in many problems. This connection is explored with interactive apps and connected to geophysical surveys.

Applications

Exploration (minerals, hydrocarbons, water, geothermal energy), environmental (salt water intrusion, contaminant spills, UXO) and geotechnical (slope stability, near-surface geology) problems are addressed through case histories, each of which is presented in a Seven- Step process.
